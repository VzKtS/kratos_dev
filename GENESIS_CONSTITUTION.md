Article I  Core Principles

Decentralization is structural, not cosmetic.

No authority is permanent.

No community is mandatory.

No failure is global.

No power accumulates without time.

Exit is a fundamental right.

Forking is legitimate.
----------------------------------------
Article II Scope of the Root Chain

The Root Chain (L0) SHALL be minimal.

It MAY provide:

   consensus and finality

   staking and slashing

   issuance and fees

   sidechain registry

   fork mechanics

   emergency coordination

It MUST NOT:

   impose ideology

   define social norms

   enforce identity

   arbitrate local governance

   prevent exit or fork
----------------------------------------
Article III  Sovereignty of Sidechains

Every sidechain is sovereign within its declared scope.

Sidechains MAY define:

   governance models

   identity systems

   economic rules

Sidechains MAY:

   federate voluntarily

   dissolve

   merge

   fork

No sidechain SHALL be permanent by default.
----------------------------------------
Article IV  Governance & Power

Governance power MUST be:

   bounded

   revocable

   slow to accumulate

Economic stake alone SHALL NOT grant absolute control.

Reputation and identity MAY influence governance only where explicitly enabled.

All governance parameters SHALL remain within constitutional bounds (SPEC v6).
----------------------------------------
Article V  Validators & Security

Validators secure the protocol in exchange for rewards.

Validators are accountable via:

   slashing

   reputation decay

   removal

No validator SHALL be irreplaceable.

Security failures SHALL be:

   detectable

   contestable

   recoverable
----------------------------------------
Article VI  Identity & Personhood

Identity is optional at the protocol level.

No global identity registry SHALL exist.

Proof of Personhood MAY exist only:

   locally

   voluntarily

   revocably

Identity SHALL NEVER be required to:

   hold assets

   transact

   exit

   fork
----------------------------------------
Article VII  Forking & Neutrality

Forking is a constitutional right.

No fork SHALL be privileged by the protocol.

Choosing a fork SHALL NOT result in:

   slashing

   confiscation

   identity loss

Assets, identities, and reputations SHALL persist across forks, subject to decay rules.

Ossification MAY occur only under:

   long-term stability

   overwhelming consensus

   continued fork possibility
----------------------------------------
Article VIII  Emergencies

Emergency powers exist solely to:

   preserve continuity

   prevent irreversible damage

Emergencies SHALL be:

   temporary

   auditable

   automatically expiring

No emergency SHALL:

   suspend exit

   bypass the Constitution

   create permanent authority
----------------------------------------
Article IX  Purge, Exit & Death

All chains MUST be allowed to die.

Purge SHALL be:

   deterministic

   rule-based

   irreversible

Exit SHALL always be possible.

No capital SHALL be frozen without a withdrawal path.
----------------------------------------
Article X  Amendments

This Constitution MAY be amended only via:

   constitutional governance (SPEC v6)

   supermajority approval

   enforced timelocks

Constitutional axioms are immutable.

Any amendment violating core principles is invalid.
----------------------------------------
Article XI  Supremacy & Interpretation

In case of conflict:

Constitution > SPECs > Contracts > Governance Decisions

Ambiguity SHALL be resolved in favor of:

   decentralization

   exit

   neutrality

No interpretation SHALL grant permanent power.

Final Declaration

KratOs does not promise harmony.
It promises continuity without coercion.

It is not designed to rule humanity,
but to allow humans to organize, disagree, separate, and persist.

Where others enforce unity, KratOs guarantees choice.

Adopted at Genesis.

End of Genesis Constitution
